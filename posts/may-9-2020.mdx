The U.S. unemployment rate hits 14.7%. With 20.5 million people out of work, unemployment was the most severe since the Great Depression.

While much of the COVID crisis had not affected my life directly, with nobody I know having contracted the virus yet, I could see the negative impact COVID-19 was having on the economy. I watched as my father started working fewer days of the week, eventually leaving his job.
